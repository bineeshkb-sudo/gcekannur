Home/Electrical & Electronics Engineering

Power systems, machines, control, and energy technologies

Established in 1986, the EEE department combines strong core engineering with research and energy-system applications.

The official EEE pages describe the department as one of the founding academic units of GCEK, built to produce technically strong engineers with practical knowledge, leadership qualities, and industry relevance.

Department Snapshot

Founding department, broad laboratory base, and accredited UG programme

The official department and research pages present EEE as a long-running department with a rich faculty mix, multiple advanced laboratories, and research-centre status under both Kannur University and APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University. The college accreditation page lists the UG programme under the 2023-2026 cycle.

Overview

Department profile based on the current GCEK site

Academic Identity

The EEE department states that it started in 1986 with the establishment of the college and has been committed to producing quality engineers with strong technical knowledge, practical grounding, leadership, and decision-making skills.

Research and Expertise

The research page says the department is an accredited research centre under both Kannur University and APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University. The same public material highlights work in power systems, power electronics and drives, control systems, and renewable energy-related areas.

Student Development

The official mission and programme statements focus on updated infrastructure, competency building, industry and research readiness, and long-term professional growth in engineering, academia, and entrepreneurship.

Programmes

Current programmes highlighted for EEE

The public GCEK programme list shows B.Tech Electrical and Electronics Engineering as the core undergraduate offering, with postgraduate programmes in Power Electronics and Drives and Power Systems under the EEE department. The college accreditation page lists the UG Electrical and Electronics Engineering programme in the 2023-2026 accreditation cycle.

Laboratories

Facilities listed in the official department pages

Core Electrical Labs

The EEE department site lists Basic Electrical Lab, Electrical Machines Lab, and Measurements and Instrumentation Lab as key facilities supporting the undergraduate curriculum.

Power and Control Labs

Power Electronics Lab, Control Systems Lab, and Advanced Power Electronics Lab support training in converters, drives, control, and modern electrical-system applications.

Advanced Facilities

The department also lists Advanced Electrical Lab and a departmental library as part of its learning and research environment.

Research Support

The official research page highlights facilities and experimental setups relevant to power electronics, control, renewable energy, and intelligent energy-system studies.
