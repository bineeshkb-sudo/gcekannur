Overview

Department profile based on the current GCEK site

Foundational Role

The official ASM page says the department was established in October 1986 along with the college and teaches fundamental subjects such as Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and Humanities to B.Tech students.

Educational Mission

The department vision and mission emphasize scientific foundation, critical and logical thinking, ethical development, communication, and the application of fundamental principles to real-world engineering situations.

Interdisciplinary Support

The information bulletin describes the department as providing intellectual support to student project work and helping encourage interest in science and engineering beyond basic classroom instruction.

Academic Role

How ASM supports the engineering curriculum

The current ASM page does not present a separate degree programme. Instead, it highlights the department's college-wide role in delivering foundational courses in Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and Humanities to engineering students, while also supporting analytical skill-building, communication, and project-related intellectual development.

Facilities

Facilities described in the public material

Science Laboratories

The ASM department supports foundational learning in Physics and Chemistry through first-year laboratory exposure linked to engineering science education.

Department Library

The information bulletin notes a departmental library with books in science, technology, and general-interest areas that support both faculty and students.

Project and Computing Support

The bulletin also refers to a project lab and academic support that helps students carry out project work and scientific computing activities more effectively.

Cross-College Contribution

Because the department serves multiple programmes, its facilities and teaching effort support the wider academic ecosystem of GCEK rather than a single branch alone.
