Department Snapshot

Founding department, strong UG intake history, and postgraduate depth

The live department profile says the UG programme began in 1986 with an intake of 40, later increased to 90 in 2000, and the PG programme in Signal Processing and Embedded Systems started in 2012 with an intake of 18. The college accreditation page lists the UG programme in the 2023-2026 cycle.

Overview

Department profile based on the current GCEK site

Academic Identity

The official ECE page says the department started in 1986 and became firmly established after the campus shifted to Mangattuparamba. It positions itself around quality electronics education supported by strong laboratory work and broad engineering fundamentals.

Programme Growth

The current public profile notes the UG intake increase to 90 in 2000 and the launch of the M.Tech programme in Signal Processing and Embedded Systems in 2012. It also notes later affiliation under APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University from 2015 admissions onward.

Student and Faculty Strengths

The official page emphasizes practical training, communication and leadership qualities, and faculty with strong academic and professional backgrounds dedicated to shaping student careers.

Programmes

Current programmes highlighted for ECE

The public GCEK programme pages present B.Tech Electronics and Communication Engineering as the core undergraduate offering and M.Tech Signal Processing and Embedded Systems as the major postgraduate programme under the department. The ECE research-supervisors page also shows multiple approved supervisors, indicating active research guidance. The college accreditation page lists the UG programme in the 2023-2026 cycle.

Laboratories

Facilities listed in the official department pages

Electronics Core Labs

The department pages list Electronic Circuits Lab, Digital Electronics Lab, and Microprocessor and Microcontroller Lab among the major foundational laboratories.

Communication and Signal Labs

Communication Engineering Lab, Communication Systems Lab, and Advanced Communication and Signal Processing Lab support communication-system understanding and advanced signal work.

Embedded and VLSI Labs

VLSI and Embedded Systems Lab and the FAB Lab support hardware-oriented design learning and prototyping exposure.

Project and Research Spaces

Simulation and Computation Lab, Project Laboratory, Seminar Hall, and Department Library help connect coursework with projects, design work, and guided research.
