Institutional profile and identity

One of Kerala's established government engineering colleges, rooted in public service and academic rigor.

Government College of Engineering, Kannur was established in 1986 and functions on a scenic 68-acre campus at Mangattuparamba, near the National Highway and about 15 km from Kannur town. The college presents itself as one of the premier institutions among Kerala's government engineering colleges.

History

Institutional journey

GCEK began in 1986 and has grown into a major public engineering institution in North Kerala. The college highlights its strong infrastructure, established faculty base, and long-standing role in engineering education under the state system.

The current profile also emphasizes the college's practical orientation, industry linkage, and student development activities alongside formal academics.

Vision & Mission

Guiding principles

Vision

To be a globally respected institution known for excellence in engineering education, research, and consultancy.

Mission

To contribute to society through quality education and training that support innovation, entrepreneurship, and sustainable growth.

Institutional Character

The current site presents GCEK as a public institution that values academic quality, research culture, employability, and all-round student development.

Campus Profile

Location, facilities, and student environment

Campus Setting

The college functions at Mangattuparamba on a large scenic campus near the National Highway, with convenient access from Kannur.

Academic Range

The current profile notes 5 B.Tech programmes along with multiple M.Tech specializations and doctoral opportunities.

Residential Facilities

The college states that it has 2 boys' hostels, 3 girls' hostels, and staff quarters within the campus ecosystem.

Student Support

The present site highlights training and placement support, PTA involvement, student union activity, and a range of co-curricular forums.

Academic Scope

Programmes, research, and institutional strengths

The current college profile says GCEK offers undergraduate programmes in Civil, Mechanical, Electrical and Electronics, Electronics and Communication, and Computer Science and Engineering. It also lists postgraduate specializations across core departments, research centres in Civil, Mechanical, and Electrical Engineering, and doctoral work under Kannur University and Kerala Technological University.

The same profile highlights recognition as a Centre of Excellence in Systems, Energy and Environment, technical collaboration with Delft University in the Netherlands, and a strong network of student forums including NSS, IEEE, ISTE, alumni activities, and innovation-oriented groups.
