A modern CSE department with UG, PG, research, and strong laboratory support.

Department Snapshot

UG intake of 60, a new AI and Data Science M.Tech in 2024, and KTU-approved research activity

The official department page says the undergraduate intake rose from 30 to 60 in 2011, the department serves as a KTU-approved research centre, and the M.Tech programme in Artificial Intelligence & Data Science admitted its inaugural batch in 2024.

Overview

Department profile based on the current GCEK site

Academic Identity

The official CSE page says the department started in 2001 and now offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral opportunities in computing. It positions itself around strong theoretical foundations and practical, application-oriented engineering education.

Research and Infrastructure

The live department profile says the department is a KTU-approved research centre. It also highlights a Central Computing Lab in the main college block, six specialized laboratories in the department building, and a departmental library with more than 2,000 books.

Student Outcomes

The current site emphasizes strong B.Tech performance, campus placements in reputed companies, higher-study progression, and active student participation in co-curricular and extra-curricular activities.

Programmes

Current programmes highlighted for CSE

The official CSE site presents B.Tech Computer Science and Engineering as the core programme, mentions doctoral research opportunities, and records the launch of the M.Tech programme in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science in 2024 with an inaugural batch of 6 students. The college accreditation page lists the UG Computer Science and Engineering programme under the 2023-2026 accreditation cycle.

Laboratories

Facilities highlighted through the department site and information bulletin

Programming Paradigms Lab

The information bulletin describes this lab as a space for programming in multiple paradigms, along with experiments related to operating systems, databases, and compiler construction.

Hardware and Embedded Systems Lab

The bulletin notes exposure to assembly language, low-level C, microprocessors, microcontrollers, FPGA boards, device interfacing, and real-time systems.

Network and Information Security Lab

The department infrastructure includes training and experimentation in computer networking, security protocols, network simulation, firewalls, IDS, cryptography, and countermeasures.

Graphics, Multimedia, and Project Spaces

The public material also points to graphics and multimedia learning facilities, a research lab, server room, and broad access to computing resources for advanced projects.
